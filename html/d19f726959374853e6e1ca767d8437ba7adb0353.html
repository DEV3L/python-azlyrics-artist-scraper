Writer(s): SWIFT TAYLOR, WHITE JOHN PAUL, WILLIAMS JOY ELIZABETH, BURNETT JOSEPH HENRY
This song was inspired by the "Hunger Games" (2012) film where Katniss Everdeen sings a lullaby to her little sister Prim.
Taylor Swift told MTV News, "I had this title that I had been working with called 'Safe & Sound' and I just knew that I wanted it to deal with the empathy, sort of the more sensitive side, the bittersweet side of this story."
This track was produced by T-Bone Burnett. When Taylor Swift heard the result she said, "When we got the track back, I was so surprised by the restraint. He created this ethereal sound without making it this big battle anthem." T. Bone Burnett was also responsible for producing the soundtrack to "O Brother Where Art Thou" (2000).
This track was released on the album "The Hunger Games: Songs From District 12 and Beyond" (2012). Another song by Taylor Swift, "Eyes Open", is also a part of this compilation.
The shooting process of the music video was commented by Taylor Swift to MTV News, "It was so cold. It was freezing. I was shaking the whole time and trying not to be shaking when the cameras were rolling. It was about 40 degrees in Nashville. It had just rained. I'm wearing this 1920s vintage nightgown that didn't provide much warmth."
