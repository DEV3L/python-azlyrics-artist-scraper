Writer(s): Tyler Joseph, Josh Dun
Tyler Joseph said about this song, "My goal is to look cool every night when I play it but I fail miserably sometimes. It's a little challenge every night. It's definitely all over the place genre-wise, but that's the point I think. The bridge still catches me off-guard. I sometimes even forget it's coming when I play it live. I absolutely love that, that I, as the songwriter and performer, have to wrap my mind around what the song is doing. That's a freaking good song I think!"
The vocalist said explained some of this song's themes in an interview about the "Vessel" album, "It's about putting on a façade for people. I think about being on stage, trying to look cool. Right now I'm wearing this really nice shirt. I don't have to wear this shirt… I'm trying to look cool so people think I'm worth listening to or something. The song touches on how we constantly have to lie to people about who we really are. It gets dangerous when you start lying to yourself."
This track was released as the album's fourth single through the iTunes Store in the Scandinavian countries on the 15th of September 2013.
The band performed "Fake You Out" during their "Quiet Is Violent World Tour" (2014), as part of a medley with a cover version of "Summertime Sadness" by Lana Del Rey.
